About

I am an incoming Ph.D. student in Intelligent Science and Technology at Fudan University, with a joint training affiliation at Beijing Zhongguancun Academy. Before joining Fudan University, I received my M.A. in Public Policy from Lanzhou University and my B.A. in Land Resource Management from Renmin University of China.

My work focuses on how environmental risks, carbon policies, energy transitions, and urban spatial structures reshape financial stability and regional development. I use quantitative methods, spatial analysis, machine learning, and network-based risk models to examine nonlinear relationships and spillover effects across cities, regions, and financial markets.

Publications

Does Cutting Carbon Emissions Reduce Tail Risk Spillovers? A Quantile LSTM-KAN-CoVaR Approach

Journal of Futures Markets, 2025. ABS 3, JCR Q2, IF = 2.3. Corresponding Author.

This paper examines the dynamic relationship between carbon emissions and tail-risk spillover networks in European futures markets. The findings suggest that oil and natural gas futures are major receivers of tail risk, while carbon emissions amplify tail-risk exposure in renewable energy futures and strengthen risk spillovers across energy futures markets.

Nonlinear Relationship between Physical Environment Risks, Investor Attentions, and Financial Systemic Risks: Evidence from mLSTM-CoVaR Networks

Journal of Environmental Management, 2025. ABS 3, IF = 8.0, JCR Q1/Q2. Corresponding Author.

This study investigates how physical environmental risks affect financial systemic risks. The results reveal significant nonlinear interactions between environmental risks and financial vulnerability. In normal market conditions, investor attention can amplify the impact of environmental risks on financial stability.

Green Finance and Micro-Zone Level Carbon Emissions

Finance Research Letters, 2025. ABS 2, IF = 7.4. Third Author.

This study analyzes the effects of green finance policies on carbon emissions across different urbanized areas. The findings show that green finance reduces emissions mainly in urban centers while potentially inducing carbon leakage toward suburban areas.
